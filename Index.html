04 — The Behavioral Science Engine

Every nudge has a
scientific reason to work.

Creditas doesn't send messages — we engineer behavioral change. Each intervention is anchored in a proven behavioral economics principle applied specifically to credit card adoption.

Behavioral economics tells us that humans are not rational actors. We make decisions based on cognitive shortcuts, emotional triggers, and contextual cues. Our program exploits these patterns — ethically and precisely — to move customers from inaction to first spend.

Status Quo Bias

People resist change

"If it ain't broke, don't fix it." Customers use existing cards not because they're better — but because switching requires effort.

Creditas applies: Guided wallet setup, one-tap card-on-file, zero-effort onboarding via in-app nudges.

Day 1–2Day 2–3
Loss Aversion

Losses feel 2× worse

Kahneman & Tversky: humans are more motivated to avoid losing ₹500 than to gain ₹500. Pain of loss outweighs equivalent reward.

Creditas applies: "Your ₹500 cashback is unclaimed." "Lounge visits expire in 5 days." Framing benefits as expiring losses.

Day 15–18Day 29–30
Endowed Progress

Completion momentum

People work harder when they've already made progress — even if that progress was given artificially. The stamp card effect.

Creditas applies: Milestone ladder starts at ₹500 — within easy reach. Once hit, momentum carries forward.

Day 15–18
Operant Conditioning

Variable rewards = habits

B.F. Skinner: unpredictable rewards create stronger behavioral loops than predictable ones. The slot machine effect.

Creditas applies: Spend streak with escalating reward reveals creates a daily habit loop anchored to the card.

Day 13–16
Hyperbolic Discounting

Small now beats large later

Humans over-value immediate rewards. A ₹200 discount today is more motivating than ₹500 points redeemable in 3 months.

Creditas applies: Gyftr Insta Vouchers provide instant, tangible value. Immediate gratification drives action.

Day 8–10Day 25–27
Scarcity

Rare things feel valuable

Cialdini: when availability is limited — by time or quantity — perceived value increases and action accelerates significantly.

Creditas applies: All offers carry hard expiry dates. Final-phase messages carry countdown timers. Urgency is manufactured deliberately.

Day 23–25Day 29–30
Social Proof

We follow the crowd

When uncertain, humans default to majority behaviour. Peers using a card is more persuasive than any feature description.

Creditas applies: Messaging references peer behaviour — normalising card usage and making dormancy feel like the exception.

Day 10–12
Identifiable Loss

Named losses hurt more

A specific, named loss carries far more weight than a vague one. "Your ₹500" hurts more than "cashback offer."

Creditas applies: All benefit reminders name the exact amount. "Your 2 lounge visits" beats "you have lounge benefits."
